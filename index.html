Understanding Nursing Bottle Caries

Nursing bottle caries (also known as baby bottle tooth decay) is a dental condition that occurs when a child's teeth are frequently exposed to sugary liquids for long periods. This common problem affects infants and toddlers who are regularly put to bed with a bottle or use a bottle as a pacifier.

The sugars in formula, milk, and juice pool around the teeth and gums, creating an ideal environment for bacteria that produce acid. This acid attacks tooth enamel, leading to decay that typically affects the upper front teeth first but can damage other teeth as well.

Risk Factors for Nursing Bottle Caries:

  • Frequent, prolonged feeding with a bottle containing sugary liquids
  • Putting a child to bed with a bottle
  • Using a bottle as a pacifier during the day
  • Not cleaning the baby's teeth after feedings
  • Extended breastfeeding on demand throughout the night
